# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The creator's own family, planning and taking an 8-day Southern California road trip in
2026 (Santa Clara → Disneyland → Los Angeles → San Diego, driving a Tesla Model 3 Long
Range). Bilingual readers (中文 primary, English secondary). They open this on a phone
while planning at home and again on the road — to pick between itinerary plans, check the
day's schedule, navigate to the next stop, find food/cafés, locate Superchargers, and tick
off a kids' packing list. They already know the context, so the site is a private companion,
not a pitch — but it should still feel special enough to be a keepsake of the trip.

## Product Purpose

A single, self-contained microsite that turns a family road trip into one delightful,
glanceable plan. It exists so the whole trip lives in one link instead of scattered notes,
maps, and chat messages. Success = the family actually uses it before and during the trip,
can switch between the A/B/C plans without friction, and finds every practical answer
(where to eat, where to charge, what to pack, how to get there) in seconds.

## Brand Personality

Sunny, playful, and warm — vacation excitement made tangible. Voice is friendly and
encouraging, bilingual without feeling like a translation. Three words: **sunny, joyful,
effortless**. The emotional goal is anticipation and ease: opening it should feel like the
trip already started, never like doing homework. Whimsy is welcome (palm trees, a countdown,
warm coastal light) but always in service of clarity.

## Anti-references

- **Generic travel-agency / booking-site templates** (Expedia, corporate stock-photo
  layouts). No sterile "deals" framing, no stocky hero imagery.
- **Cluttered 小红书 screenshot-collage energy** — emoji-soup, over-stuffed cards, anything
  that's hard to scan. Emoji are accents, not the layout.
- **Cold SaaS / admin-dashboard look** — sterile app-UI chrome with no warmth or character.

## Design Principles

- **One link, the whole trip.** Everything the family needs lives here; never make them
  leave to find an answer. Outbound links (maps nav, 小红书) are shortcuts, not dependencies.
- **Glanceable first, deep on demand.** Each section answers its question in seconds;
  detail collapses/expands rather than crowding the page.
- **Joy in service of clarity.** Warmth, motion, and whimsy earn their place only when they
  help the reader feel oriented and excited — never as decoration that slows scanning.
- **Bilingual as one voice.** 中文 and English coexist as a single designed system, with a
  clear typographic hierarchy between them — not a bolt-on translation.
- **Built for the road.** Mobile-first, fast on a single self-contained file, legible in
  bright sunlight and at night (light + dark themes).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast in both light and dark themes — watch the
muted `--ink-soft` on the warm `--paper` background and the colored chips/badges. Respect
`prefers-reduced-motion` for the countdown, reveals, and card animations. Tap targets ≥44px
for on-the-road one-handed use. Maintain `lang` attributes so screen readers switch voices
between 中文 and English. Don't rely on color alone for plan/heat/badge distinctions.
